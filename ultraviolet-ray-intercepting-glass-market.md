<p><p>Ultraviolet Ray Intercepting Glass is categorized into two main types: Monolayer and Double Layer. Monolayer glass features a single coating that effectively blocks UV rays while allowing visible light to pass through. This type is often used in architectural applications where moderate UV protection is needed. In contrast, Double Layer glass includes two coatings, providing enhanced UV filtration and better thermal insulation. This type is favored in high-performance settings, such as in museums or galleries, to protect sensitive materials from UV damage.</p></p>
