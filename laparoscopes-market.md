<p><ul><li>Rigid</li><li>Flexible</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The laparoscope market is divided into two main types: rigid and flexible laparoscopes. Rigid laparoscopes are commonly used in minimally invasive surgeries due to their fixed design, providing excellent visualization and stability. They are typically employed in procedures like gallbladder removals and hernia repairs. In contrast, flexible laparoscopes offer enhanced maneuverability, allowing surgeons to navigate complex anatomical structures more easily. These are particularly useful in procedures requiring access to difficult-to-reach areas, adding versatility to surgical options.</p></p>
